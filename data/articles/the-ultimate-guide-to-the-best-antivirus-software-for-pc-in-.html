Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between antivirus software and anti-malware software?

Antivirus software primarily focuses on identifying and removing viruses, which are a type of malware that replicates itself to spread to other computers. Anti-malware software, on the other hand, targets a broader range of threats, including spyware, adware, and other malicious programs that can harm your PC. While the terms are often used interchangeably, anti-malware software is generally considered a more comprehensive solution.

Can antivirus software protect against ransomware?

Yes, modern antivirus software is designed to detect and prevent ransomware attacks, which involve encrypting your files and demanding payment in exchange for the decryption key. Many top-rated antivirus programs include advanced ransomware protection features, such as real-time monitoring, behavioral detection, and automatic backup capabilities. However, no antivirus software can guarantee complete protection against ransomware, as new variants can emerge at any time.
